# LifeZen Support

LifeZen is an OpenZen project that helps people sort waste and recycling with less confusion.

The goal is simple: make everyday disposal decisions clearer, explain why an item belongs in a certain bin or recycling stream, and help people build better habits without turning sustainability into a lecture.

## What LifeZen is for

LifeZen is designed for everyday questions such as:

- Which bin should this item go into?
- Can this packaging be recycled?
- What should I do with mixed materials?
- Why is this item not accepted in the usual bin?
- How can I avoid common sorting mistakes?

LifeZen focuses on practical guidance, plain explanations, and a calm user experience.

## Part of OpenZen

OpenZen builds practical AI products and privacy-first digital tools for real-world tasks.

Our products are small, focused, and designed around clear user needs:

- HelpZen — a privacy-first help navigator for people in Germany.

## Support

If you found a problem, have a question, or want to suggest an improvement, please open an issue in this repository:

https://github.com/Arentai86/lifezen-support/issues

Helpful reports include:

- the item or material you asked about;
- the answer you expected;
- what LifeZen answered instead;
- your country or region, if local rules matter;
- screenshots, if relevant.

Please do not post private documents, exact home addresses, personal IDs, payment data, or sensitive information in public issues.

## Privacy-first approach

LifeZen should help without collecting more information than needed.

OpenZen’s product direction is based on:

- clear interfaces;
- practical explanations;
- human control over AI suggestions;
- careful handling of user data;
- no fake certainty when local rules are unclear.

Waste and recycling rules can vary by municipality. LifeZen should explain uncertainty clearly and encourage users to check local rules when needed.

## Product status

This repository is the public support and feedback space for LifeZen.

It is not a place for secret keys, private user data, internal infrastructure, or production incidents containing sensitive information.
